Responsibilities and achievements

I have been doing research since 2014. In 2019, I got the chance to spend almost two years at the University of Jyväskylä as a grant researcher. I have been in contact with the university since 2012, when I had the opportunity to work at the former TAIKU for a semester as a teacher trainee. Since then, I have been also taking part in editing a periodics, Spectrum Hungarologicum, which is published by the University of Jyväskylä and the University of Pécs. As a researcher, I focus on literary trauma studies in the context of contemporary Hungarian literature. I analyse narratives that deal with individual and collective memory of the Communist era and tackle mental trauma caused by political oppression and systematic victimization. Among other fields, my topic is in close connection with creative writing as well due to the potential therapeutic use of trauma narratives.

Responsibilities and achievements

I worked as an Assistant Professor between 2014 and 2017 at Hankuk University of Foreign Studies in South Korea. I was a member of the Department of Hungarian Studies and my main duty was teaching Hungarian as a second language. In our Bachelor program, our students had the chance to gain advance knowledge on the language and the culture as well.
